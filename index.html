The best way to describe myself as a Software Engineer is someone who builds new things to solve old problems. I thrive in environments where I’ve got the freedom to fix real issues and suggest smarter ways of doing things. I’ve worked across backend, frontend, and data engineering — but I don’t limit myself to any stack. If there’s a problem worth solving, I’ll pick up whatever I need to get it done, even if it’s weird, new, or totally outside my comfort zone.

When I'm free you'll find me tinkering with code(I'm working on building a custom clock) in more novel settings, playing soulslikes or practicing classic guitar.

My .Net journey started in May 2021 at SETIC/RO, where I interned on the dev team and worked on a monolith called SISNE (the State's appointment/dismissal system). Up until then, I only had Java EE experience, so I was pretty happy learning .Net.

By May 2022, I moved to the Data Department, still at SETIC, without dropping .Net. My capstone project — an IT Help Desk automation platform — featured a custom orchestrator and 3 .Net APIs (including a SOAP integration with a legacy system). It’s automatically solved over 40k tickets so far, saving about $10 per ticket and cutting resolution time by more than 99%.

By December 2023, I became the founder and Tech Lead of the Automation & Data Engineering team, briefly stepping away from .NET. That changed in April 2024, when SETUR (Tourism State Department) asked for a WhatsApp Chatbot — I built the MVP in .NET, anticipating wider demand. By May 2025, we launched a full SaaS Platform based on that MVP, adaptable to any State Government department. The State had originally planned to buy a similar solution for $200k USD from a vendor later involved in legal issues — by building our own, we saved $200k USD and avoided the fallout.
